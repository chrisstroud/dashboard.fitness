# Tongkat Ali (Eurycoma longifolia) Research

**Last updated:** 2026-03-31
**Status:** Under evaluation
**Bottom line:** Moderate evidence for testosterone support in hypogonadal/stressed men. Weaker evidence in healthy eugonadal men. Notable EFSA genotoxicity concern. Complementary to (not redundant with) Ashwagandha.

---

## 1. Mechanism of Action

Tongkat Ali's bioactive compounds work through several proposed pathways:

**Primary active compounds:** Quassinoids (especially eurycomanone), eurypeptides (36-amino-acid polypeptides), glycosaponins, alkaloids, and phenolic derivatives. Over 65 compounds have been identified in root extracts.

**Proposed mechanisms:**

- **Aromatase inhibition** -- Eurycomanone inhibits aromatase conversion of testosterone to estrogen at Leydig cells, leading to higher circulating testosterone. This is probably the best-supported mechanism. (Low et al., 2013, *J Ethnopharmacol*)
- **CYP17 enzyme activation** -- Eurypeptides may activate 17-alpha-hydroxylase/17,20-lyase, enhancing conversion of pregnenolone and progesterone into DHEA and androstenedione (upstream testosterone precursors)
- **SHBG reduction** -- Proposed to release free testosterone from sex hormone-binding globulin, increasing bioavailable testosterone. Evidence for this is mostly mechanistic/in vitro rather than confirmed in robust human trials.
- **HPA axis modulation** -- Reduces cortisol, possibly by modulating the stress response. This may indirectly support testosterone by reducing cortisol's suppressive effect on the HPG axis.
- **Phosphodiesterase inhibition** -- At high concentrations, eurycomanone may inhibit PDE, though this is more relevant to erectile function than testosterone per se.

**Honest assessment:** The aromatase inhibition pathway has the strongest mechanistic support. The SHBG and CYP17 claims are more speculative and largely extrapolated from in vitro work. The cortisol-lowering effect is supported by at least one human RCT.

## 2. Key Clinical Trials and RCTs

### 2a. Systematic Review & Meta-Analysis (2022) -- The Best Overview

**Leisegang et al. (2022).** "Eurycoma longifolia (Jack) Improves Serum Total Testosterone in Men: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis of Clinical Trials." *Medicina*, 58(8), 1047.

- 9 studies in systematic review, 5 RCTs in meta-analysis
- **Total n = 267 men** (135 treatment, 132 control)
- Dosages: 100-600 mg/day for 3 days to 6 months
- 7 of 9 studies used Physta extract
- **Result:** Significant increase in total testosterone (SMD = 1.352, 95% CI 0.565-2.138, p = 0.001)
- **Hypogonadal subgroup:** Stronger effect (SMD = 1.861, p = 0.002)
- **Healthy/eugonadal men:** Effect NOT statistically significant (p = 0.249)
- **Limitations:** Small samples, high heterogeneity (I-squared = 87.27%), publication bias detected, most studies used the same branded extract

**Key takeaway:** The testosterone effect is real but concentrated in men with low/borderline testosterone. If your T is already healthy, don't expect much.

### 2b. Stress Hormones & Cortisol (2013)

**Talbott et al. (2013).** "Effect of Tongkat Ali on stress hormones and psychological mood state in moderately stressed subjects." *J Int Soc Sports Nutr*, 10, 28.

- Randomized, placebo-controlled, n = 63 (32 men, 31 women)
- 200 mg/day Physta for 4 weeks
- **Cortisol:** Reduced 16% vs placebo
- **Testosterone:** Increased 37% vs placebo
- **Cortisol:Testosterone ratio:** Improved 36%
- Mood improvements: tension (-11%), anger (-12%), confusion (-15%)

**Caveat:** The 37% testosterone increase is the most-cited number in marketing but came from a moderately stressed population, not a general healthy population. The cortisol reduction is the more consistently replicated finding.

### 2c. 6-Month ADAM Trial (2021) -- Longest RCT

**Henkel et al. (2021).** "A 6-month, double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomized trial..." *Maturitas*, 145:78-85.

- n = 45 men with androgen deficiency of aging males (ADAM), age ~47
- 4 arms: placebo, tongkat ali only (200 mg/day), exercise only, tongkat ali + exercise
- 6 months duration (longest RCT to date)
- **Results:** Tongkat ali increased testosterone in ~50% of participants. Best results in the combined tongkat ali + exercise group: improved erectile function, increased total testosterone, decreased body fat, improved VO2 peak.
- **Limitation:** Very small per-group sample (~11 per arm)

### 2d. Body Composition in Trained Individuals (2024) -- Negative Result

**Escalante et al. (2024).** "The Effect of Tongkat Ali Supplementation on Body Composition in Exercise-Trained Males and Females." *Applied Sciences*, 14(11), 4372.

- RCT, n = 33 exercise-trained males and females
- 400 mg/day for 4 weeks
- **Result:** No significant effect on body mass, lean mass, fat mass, body fat %, handgrip strength, mood, sleep, free testosterone, or cortisol
- **Interpretation:** In already-fit, presumably eugonadal individuals, 4 weeks of tongkat ali did nothing measurable. This aligns with the meta-analysis showing no significant effect in healthy men.

### 2e. Physta Testosterone & Quality of Life (2012)

**Ismail et al. (2012).** "Randomised clinical trial on the use of Physta on testosterone levels and quality of life in ageing male subjects." *Food & Nutrition Research*.

- n = 76 late-onset hypogonadal men
- 200 mg/day Physta for 1 month
- Significant increases in total and free testosterone
- AMS (Aging Males' Symptoms) scores improved

### 2f. Cyclists & Endurance (2003)

**Ooi et al.** Tongkat ali in endurance cyclists showed 32% lower cortisol and 16% higher testosterone vs placebo, supporting the anti-catabolic/stress-buffering hypothesis.

## 3. Dosing

### What the studies used:
| Dose | Duration | Population | Outcome |
|------|----------|------------|---------|
| 200 mg/day | 4 weeks | Stressed adults | Cortisol -16%, T +37% |
| 200 mg/day | 1 month | Hypogonadal men | Increased total + free T |
| 200 mg/day | 6 months | ADAM men | Increased T in ~50% |
| 400 mg/day | 4 weeks | Trained adults | No significant effects |
| 400 mg/day | 5 weeks | Active seniors | Some strength improvements |
| 600 mg/day | 3 weeks | Various | Safe, increased T |

### Standard recommendation:
- **200-400 mg/day** of a standardized extract (Physta or LJ100)
- Take early in the day (mild stimulant effect reported anecdotally)
- Most studies show effects emerging at 2-4 weeks
- The 6-month ADAM trial is the only long-duration data point

### Cycling:
- **Not clearly necessary at 200-400 mg/day.** Most clinical trials used continuous daily dosing without cycling.
- Huberman takes it continuously without cycling. Dr. Annie George (Biotropics, lead Tongkat Ali researcher) agrees cycling is unnecessary at standard doses.
- If using 600+ mg/day, consider 4 weeks on / 1 week off or similar prudent cycling.
- No human data specifically tests cycling vs. continuous use.

## 4. Standardized Extracts

### Physta (Biotropics Malaysia)
- Freeze-dried hot-water extract
- Standardized to 0.8-1.5% eurycomanone
- **Used in 7 of 9 studies** in the 2022 meta-analysis -- by far the most researched
- This is the extract with the strongest clinical backing
- Manufactured by Biotropics Malaysia Berhad

### LJ100 (HP Ingredients / Nusapure)
- Standardized to 40% glycosaponins, 22% eurypeptides, 1% eurycomanone
- Developed by MIT (Malaysia Institute of Technology) researchers
- Claims 25+ published studies (though many overlap with Physta research)
- Higher standardization of eurypeptides than generic extracts
- Common in US supplement market (Double Wood, Nootropics Depot, etc.)

### Quality control warning:
The 2022 meta-analysis noted that of 41 Tongkat Ali products analyzed on the market, only 11 of 24 tested met their stated eurycomanone standards. Many cheap extracts are adulterated or under-dosed. Stick with Physta or LJ100 branded extracts, or buy from vendors who publish third-party COAs.

## 5. Safety Profile

### Generally reported side effects (uncommon at standard doses):
- GI discomfort (nausea, diarrhea)
- Headache
- Rash/itching
- Mild insomnia (likely related to stimulant-like properties)
- Restlessness

### Liver toxicity:
- LiverTox (NIH) rates tongkat ali as Category D: "possible rare cause" of liver injury
- One published case report: 47-year-old bodybuilder developed jaundice and ALT of 470 U/L within 1 week of starting tongkat ali. Resolved after discontinuation. However, the case is weakened by possible undisclosed anabolic steroid use.
- Rodent studies up to 2 g/kg/day for 13 weeks showed no hepatotoxicity
- Clinical trials at 200-600 mg/day showed normal liver and renal function markers

### THE BIG CONCERN -- EFSA Genotoxicity Finding (2021):

**EFSA Panel (2021).** "Safety of Eurycoma longifolia (Tongkat Ali) root extract as a novel food." *EFSA Journal*, 19(12), e06937.

- In vitro chromosome aberration test: **positive for clastogenic (chromosome-damaging) properties** in human lymphocytes
- In vivo comet assay in rats at 2,000 mg/kg: **statistically significant DNA damage** in stomach and duodenal tissues
- Panel concluded the positive results were "due to genotoxicity rather than cytotoxicity"
- Could not identify which specific compound(s) caused the damage
- **Final conclusion: "The safety of the NF has not been established under any condition of use."**

**Context and caveats:**
- The 2,000 mg/kg dose in rats is astronomically higher than any human dose (~140,000 mg for a 70 kg human vs. typical 200-400 mg)
- The genotoxicity was most pronounced at "first sites of contact" (GI tract), suggesting direct tissue exposure matters
- This finding has NOT been replicated in other labs or in human studies
- Malaysian and Southeast Asian regulatory bodies continue to approve tongkat ali
- But the EFSA finding is not nothing -- it's a legitimate red flag that warrants monitoring

### Contraindications:
- Pregnant/lactating women (no safety data)
- Children (no safety data)
- Pre-existing liver or kidney disease
- Breast or prostate cancer (theoretical hormone-sensitive cancer concern)
- Diabetes medications (may interact with metformin, glipizide, miglitol)
- Immunosuppressants (possible interaction)
- Propranolol (possible interaction)

### Long-term safety:
- The longest RCT is 6 months. No long-term (1+ year) safety data exists in controlled trials.
- Huberman reports years of continuous use with regular bloodwork monitoring and no issues, but n=1 anecdote.

## 6. Tongkat Ali vs. Ashwagandha (KSM-66)

| Factor | Tongkat Ali | Ashwagandha (KSM-66) |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| **Primary mechanism for T** | Aromatase inhibition, SHBG reduction | Cortisol reduction (indirect T support) |
| **Testosterone effect** | 15-37% increases (mostly in low-T/stressed men) | 10-17% increases (mostly via stress reduction) |
| **Cortisol reduction** | ~16% (one RCT) | 15-28% (multiple RCTs, stronger evidence) |
| **Strength/body comp** | Weak/mixed evidence | Moderate evidence for strength gains (Wankhede 2015) |
| **Anxiety/stress** | Some mood improvements | Strong evidence for anxiety reduction |
| **Sleep** | No meaningful data | Improves sleep quality (multiple studies) |
| **Libido/sexual function** | Moderate evidence (especially in ADAM) | Some evidence, less specific |
| **Number of human RCTs** | ~10-15 | 24+ (KSM-66 specifically) |
| **Safety concerns** | EFSA genotoxicity flag | Rare liver toxicity cases; thyroid stimulation |
| **Research depth** | Moderate -- mostly one research group/extract | Deeper and more diverse research base |

### Are they complementary or redundant?

**Complementary, not redundant.** They work through different primary mechanisms:
- Tongkat ali is more directly androgenic (aromatase inhibition, SHBG)
- Ashwagandha is primarily adaptogenic (cortisol/HPA axis, with downstream T effects)
- Stacking them is common in the supplement industry. Physta + KSM-66 is the most evidence-backed combination.
- No clinical trials have tested the combination head-to-head against either alone.

### If you had to pick one:
- **Tongkat Ali** if primary goal is testosterone optimization and you have low/borderline T
- **Ashwagandha KSM-66** if primary goals include stress management, sleep, anxiety, AND some testosterone support
- **Both** if you want to cover both pathways and are willing to accept the cost and the EFSA uncertainty on tongkat ali

## 7. Notable Expert Opinions

### Andrew Huberman (Stanford neuroscientist)
- **Recommends** 400 mg/day taken early in the day
- Takes it continuously without cycling, has done so for years
- Monitors via regular bloodwork (liver enzymes, hormones)
- Previously recommended stacking with Fadogia agrestis (has since backed off Fadogia due to safety concerns and thin evidence)
- Emphasizes the SHBG-lowering / free testosterone mechanism
- **Caveat:** Huberman is not an endocrinologist and his supplement recommendations have drawn criticism for outpacing the evidence

### Rhonda Patrick (FoundMyFitness)
- Called Tongkat Ali "one of the few testosterone-boosting supplements that shows promise"
- Cited 200-400 mg/day for 1-3 months elevating testosterone 30-40% and normalizing levels in hypogonadal men
- Noted the effect is particularly relevant for those with low/borderline T, older adults, or those under stress
- More measured than Huberman -- frames it as "shows promise" rather than a strong recommendation

### Layne Norton (PhD, BioLayne)
- Has discussed tongkat ali on Muscle College Radio but has not made it a major recommendation
- Generally skeptical of "natural testosterone boosters" and emphasizes that most have overhyped effects
- Would likely categorize tongkat ali as "promising but needs more evidence"
- His supplement stack focuses on creatine, caffeine, and well-established compounds

### EFSA Panel
- Explicitly cautious. Concluded safety has "not been established under any condition of use" based on genotoxicity data. This is the most conservative institutional position.

### Malaysian researchers (Annie George, Biotropics)
- Most bullish. Have produced the majority of the clinical research. Note that most studies are funded by or connected to the company that sells Physta extract -- a significant conflict of interest to be aware of.

## 8. Strength of Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence Strength | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Increases total testosterone in hypogonadal men | **Moderate** | Meta-analysis supports, but small samples |
| Increases testosterone in healthy eugonadal men | **Weak** | Meta-analysis subgroup was NOT significant |
| Reduces cortisol | **Moderate** (one good RCT) | Talbott 2013, needs replication |
| Improves body composition | **Weak** | 2024 RCT in trained individuals was negative |
| Improves strength/performance | **Weak** | Inconsistent results, short study durations |
| Improves erectile function | **Moderate** | Henkel 2021 (6-month trial) |
| Safe at 200-400 mg/day short term | **Moderate** | No serious adverse events in trials up to 6 months |
| Safe long-term | **Unknown** | No controlled data beyond 6 months; EFSA genotoxicity concern |
| Reduces SHBG / increases free T | **Weak** | Mostly mechanistic, not well-confirmed in human RCTs |

## 9. My Decision Framework

**Arguments for adding Tongkat Ali:**
- If current T is low or borderline (<450 ng/dL), the evidence is most relevant
- If under chronic stress, the cortisol-lowering effect is plausible and complementary to Ashwagandha
- Physta and LJ100 are well-characterized extracts with decent (if limited) clinical backing
- Huberman and Rhonda Patrick both view it favorably

**Arguments against or for caution:**
- EFSA genotoxicity finding is a legitimate concern, even if the dose was extreme
- Most positive research comes from one research group with financial ties to Physta
- If T is already healthy, the meta-analysis says the effect is not significant
- No long-term safety data
- Supplement quality control is poor -- many products are adulterated

**If adding:** Use Physta or LJ100 extract, 200-400 mg/day, taken in the morning. Get baseline bloodwork (total T, free T, SHBG, liver enzymes) before starting and recheck at 8-12 weeks. Monitor liver enzymes periodically.
