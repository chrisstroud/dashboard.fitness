# Health Maxing — Research Plan

**Goal:** Comprehensive optimization of health, performance, and longevity entering age 40. Find the gaps, close them, maintain what's working.

**Subject:** Chris Stroud — 39, turning 40. Strength-focused training 4x/week + 2 cardio. Supplement stack in place. Sleep dialed. Nutrition clean. Wants to stay off TRT. Entering a new season that demands peak performance.

**Trusted Sources:** Rhonda Patrick (FoundMyFitness), Peter Attia (The Drive), Andrew Huberman (Huberman Lab), Tim Ferriss (The Tim Ferriss Show) + primary literature (PubMed, PMC).

**Method:** Research each domain below, produce a standalone research doc per domain, then synthesize into actionable protocol updates.

---

## What's Already Covered (No Research Needed)

- Resistance training programming (strength phase, progressive overload)
- Cardio structure (HIIT + Zone 2)
- Supplement stack (creatine, fish oil, D3+K2, zinc, boron, mag, L-theanine, ashwagandha)
- Sleep architecture (9 PM bed, 5 AM wake, 8 hours, bedroom optimized)
- Nutrition quality (whole foods, no seed oils, home cooking, high protein)
- Testosterone optimization (lifestyle-based, comprehensive protocol in place)
- Omega-3 deep dive (dosing, quality, product selection)
- Training science (resistance + aerobic, evidence-based protocols)

## Research Domains

### Domain 1: Bloodwork & Biomarker Strategy

**Why:** Chris has no baseline bloodwork. Can't optimize what you can't measure. Every trusted source (Attia, Patrick, Huberman) considers regular bloodwork foundational.

**Research Questions:**
- What is Peter Attia's recommended comprehensive male panel at 40? (He's the most detailed on this)
- What is the optimal testing cadence? (Quarterly, biannual, annual by marker)
- Which direct-to-consumer labs are best? (Ulta Lab Tests, LabCorp OnDemand, InsideTracker, Marek Health)
- What biomarkers should be tracked longitudinally vs. one-time baseline?
- What are optimal ranges vs. just "normal" ranges? (Attia's "Medicine 3.0" framework — optimizing, not just screening for disease)
- ApoB, Lp(a), and early cardiovascular risk — what should a 40-year-old man know?

**Output:** `Bloodwork Strategy.md` — comprehensive panel, where to order, target ranges, testing schedule.

---

### Domain 2: Cognitive Performance & Brain Health

**Why:** "On my game" isn't just physical. Chris is entering a demanding season. Cognitive sharpness, focus, executive function, and stress resilience matter as much as strength.

**Research Questions:**
- Huberman's protocols for focus, dopamine, and sustained attention — what's evidence-based vs. anecdotal?
- Attia's framework on cognitive decline prevention starting at 40 (Alzheimer's risk reduction)
- Role of BDNF — what actually increases it? (Exercise type, omega-3s, heat exposure, sleep)
- Nootropic landscape — what has real evidence? (Caffeine optimization, creatine for cognition already in stack, anything else worth considering?)
- Does Chris's current stack already cover cognitive bases? (Creatine 10g, fish oil, mag, ashwagandha, sleep)
- Digital hygiene — Ferriss/Huberman protocols for attention management, phone discipline, deep work
- Meditation — Chris does TM daily. What does the evidence say about TM vs. other modalities for cognitive performance?

**Output:** `Cognitive Performance.md` — what's already covered by current protocol, specific gaps, actionable additions.

---

### Domain 3: Cardiovascular Health & Longevity Markers

**Why:** Heart disease is the #1 killer of men. Attia calls cardiovascular disease "the most important disease to prevent" and dedicates more attention to it than anything else. Chris is 40 — the interventions made now have the highest ROI.

**Research Questions:**
- Attia's ApoB and Lp(a) framework — why these matter more than standard lipid panels
- Resting heart rate, HRV, and what Whoop data actually tells you about cardiovascular health
- Zone 2 training dose-response — is Chris doing enough? (Already addressed partially, but deeper dive on Attia's specific recommendations)
- VO2 max testing — should Chris get a baseline VO2 max test? Where? What does the number mean practically?
- Blood pressure optimization — what's the target beyond "normal"?
- Sauna and cardiovascular outcomes — the Finnish sauna studies (for when Chris gets access)
- Does Chris's current program align with Attia's "Centenarian Decathlon" framework?

**Output:** `Cardiovascular & Longevity.md` — risk assessment framework, what to test, what to track, protocol alignment.

---

### Domain 4: Hormonal Health Beyond Testosterone

**Why:** T is one hormone. The endocrine system is interconnected. Thyroid, insulin sensitivity, cortisol rhythm, growth hormone, DHEA — all shift at 40 and all affect performance.

**Research Questions:**
- Thyroid function at 40 — what to test, what to watch for, how training and nutrition affect it
- Insulin sensitivity and glucose management — Attia's CGM (continuous glucose monitor) approach. Is it worth doing a 2-week CGM experiment?
- Cortisol rhythm — Chris has ashwagandha, cyclic sighing, sleep dialed. Is there more to do here?
- Growth hormone — natural optimization vs. peptides (Sermorelin is in the T research doc). What's the honest evidence?
- DHEA-S — marker of adrenal health, declines with age. Worth tracking?
- Estrogen management in men — aromatase, body fat, and the feedback loop (already in T research, but worth expanding)

**Output:** `Hormonal Health.md` — comprehensive male hormonal landscape at 40, what to test, what to optimize naturally.

### Domain 5: Recovery & Injury Prevention

**Why:** Chris is rebuilding squat and deadlift post-injury. At 40, recovery becomes the limiting factor, not effort. The ability to train consistently without breaking down is the meta-skill.

**Research Questions:**
- Attia's approach to stability and injury prevention (DNS, functional movement)
- ATG (Knees Over Toes) — Chris already uses this as warm-up. Is there more to integrate?
- Soft tissue work — foam rolling, massage, percussion guns. What actually has evidence?
- Sleep and recovery quantification — what Whoop metrics matter for recovery decisions?
- Deload protocols — how often, how much volume reduction, what the evidence says
- Joint health supplements — collagen, glucosamine, MSM, hyaluronic acid. Any of these worth adding?
- Cold exposure for recovery — Chris has cold showers in T protocol. Timing matters (not after lifting). What's the optimal protocol?
- Training around injuries at 40 — how to auto-regulate when something doesn't feel right

**Output:** `Recovery & Injury Prevention.md` — evidence-based recovery stack, deload framework, injury prevention protocols.

### Domain 6: Gut Health & Digestion

**Why:** Already flagged in T research — gut health affects hormone absorption, supplement efficacy, inflammation, and immune function. The "unsexy" domain that affects everything.

**Research Questions:**
- Patrick's gut health recommendations — 30+ plants/week, fermented foods daily (Stanford 2021 RCT)
- Attia's perspective on gut testing (GI-MAP, etc.) — useful or waste of money?
- Prebiotics vs. probiotics vs. fermented foods — what's the hierarchy?
- Is Chris absorbing his supplements optimally? (Zinc, magnesium, D3 all depend on gut health)
- Fiber intake — is Chris hitting 25-35g/day? Does his current diet support this?
- Food sensitivities and elimination protocols — worth exploring at 40?
- L. reuteri (already on considering list) — deeper dive on the evidence

**Output:** `Gut Health.md` — audit of current diet for gut support, actionable additions, testing recommendations.

### Domain 7: Skin, Appearance & Aging

**Why:** Chris already has retinol + moisturizer in his evening routine. "On my game" includes looking sharp. Aging accelerates visibly at 40 — sun damage, collagen loss, skin quality.

- Collagen supplementation — does it work or is it just expensive protein?
- Red light therapy for skin (already in T research doc — strong evidence for collagen production)
- Sun exposure balance — morning sunlight for circadian rhythm vs. UV damage and aging
- Hair health and maintenance at 40 — what actually works beyond finasteride?
- Dental health as a longevity marker (Chris already has a floss + brush routine)

**Output:** `Appearance & Aging.md` — current routine audit, evidence-based additions, product recommendations.

### Domain 8: Stress, Mental Health & Mindset

**Why:** A new demanding season of life. Physical optimization means nothing if mental health isn't supported. Ferriss and Huberman both emphasize this as foundational.

**Research Questions:**
- Ferriss's "fear-setting" and stoic practices — evidence base for stress inoculation
- Huberman's dopamine management framework — avoiding dopamine troughs, protecting motivation
- Journaling and reflection practices — what formats have evidence? (Morning pages, gratitude, etc.)
- Social connection and testosterone/longevity — the data on isolation vs. community
- THC use (Chris uses before 6 PM cutoff) — what does the evidence say about cannabis and testosterone, sleep architecture, cognitive performance, motivation?
- Breathwork protocols beyond cyclic sighing — is Chris's current practice sufficient?
- TM meditation — Chris already does this daily. Evidence for cognitive and stress benefits.

**Output:** `Stress & Mental Performance.md` — current practices audit, evidence-based additions, decision on THC.
