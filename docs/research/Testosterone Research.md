**Evidence key:** Strong = multiple RCTs or meta-analyses | Moderate = limited RCTs or observational | Weak = animal studies or single small trials

## Medical Interventions

### Daily Tadalafil 5mg (Cialis)

**Evidence: Strong.**

PDE5 inhibitor — keeps blood vessels dilated body-wide. FDA-approved for daily use at 5mg.

- Multiple RCTs + meta-analysis (1,596 patients across 6 trials)
- Real vascular benefit: 8.3% improvement in arterial dilation
- Better blood flow to working muscle = better nutrient delivery, better pump
- Does NOT raise testosterone directly — it's a blood flow tool
- Side effects: mild headache, nasal congestion, flushing — diminish over time
- Contraindicated with nitrates

**Access:** Telehealth same-day prescription (Hims, Roman, Ro). Generic tadalafil is $0.50-$2/pill. ~$30-60/month.

### Sermorelin (GH Secretagogue)

**Evidence: Moderate.**

Synthetic GHRH that stimulates your pituitary to release growth hormone in natural pulses.

- Still legal to compound (unlike CJC-1295/Ipamorelin, banned Aug 2024)
- Expect 3-6 months before noticing meaningful changes (sleep quality, modest fat loss, recovery)
- 100-300 mcg subcutaneous nightly, 5 days on / 2 days off
- Side effects: water retention, occasional headache — generally mild
- Cost: $150-225/month via telehealth (Ivy Rx, Strut Health, Eden, Defy Medical)

### Tesamorelin

**Evidence: Strong for visceral fat reduction.**

FDA-approved GHRH analogue with Phase III RCT data. Demonstrably reduces visceral fat and increases lean mass. Some clinics prescribe off-label. $200-400/month.

### TRT — When to Consider

Only if total testosterone is **below 300 ng/dL** with symptoms, confirmed on **two** separate morning draws before 10 AM.

Modern protocol: testosterone cypionate, 100-150 mg/week split into 2x subQ injections. Monitor hematocrit, estradiol, and PSA every 3 months first year.

Trade-offs: suppresses fertility (add HCG 250-500 IU EOD to preserve), elevates hematocrit (donate blood), may need estrogen management.

**Clinics:** Marek Health (comprehensive, $250-400/mo), Defy Medical (physician-led), Maximus Tribe ($120-250/mo, offers enclomiphene — non-suppressive option), Hone Health ($45 at-home test kit to get started).

## Body Fat & Aromatase

**Evidence: Strong. The most directly actionable lever for many men.**

Aromatase is an enzyme in fat tissue that converts testosterone to estrogen. More fat = more conversion = lower T.

> High body fat → more aromatase → more T→estrogen conversion → lower T → favors fat storage → repeat

- **Optimal body fat for testosterone: 8-15%**
- Below 8%: body interprets as famine, downregulates T
- Above 15%: aromatase activity rises meaningfully
- Above 25%: clinically significant hypogonadism is common
- **Waist circumference above 40" is an independent predictor of low T**

**Recomp strategy at ~20% body fat:**
- Moderate deficit only (300-500 cal/day) — aggressive cutting suppresses T
- Prioritize compound strength training (acutely raises T, reduces aromatase activity)
- Protein: 0.8-1g per lb bodyweight — non-negotiable for muscle preservation
- Zone 2 cardio 2-3x/week uniquely targets visceral fat without cortisol penalty
- Reduce fructose/sugar (preferentially converts to visceral fat)
- At 15%, transition to maintenance/slight surplus

## Gut Health & Testosterone

**Evidence: Moderate (mechanism is strong, human outcome data is emerging)**

- Gut microbes influence GnRH/LH release (the cascade that triggers T synthesis)
- Specific bacteria have enzymes that directly metabolize androgens
- Compromised gut barrier → LPS leakage → systemic inflammation → cortisol up → T down
- Poor gut = poor absorption of zinc, D3, magnesium (all T cofactors)

**What to do:**
- 30+ different plant foods per week (highest ROI gut intervention, costs nothing)
- Fermented foods daily: kimchi, sauerkraut, kefir, plain yogurt (Stanford 2021 RCT: fermented foods > high-fiber diet alone for microbiome diversity)
- Reduce ultra-processed foods (primary driver of dysbiosis)
- **L. reuteri ATCC PTA 6475** (BioGaia Gastrus): mouse study showed maintained youthful T in aging mice. Human data promising but not definitive. ~$30-40/month. Strain specificity matters.

## Myostatin & Epicatechin

**Evidence: Moderate for biomarkers, Weak for actual muscle mass in healthy adults**

Myostatin limits muscle growth. Follistatin neutralizes it.

**Epicatechin** (cocoa flavanol):
- 8-week RCT in older males: training + epicatechin significantly increased follistatin/myostatin ratio and strength vs. training alone
- Caveat: most studies are in sarcopenic older adults, not healthy trained men
- One cycling study: 200mg/day *inhibited* aerobic adaptations
- Dose: 150-200mg/day from extract
- Cost: ~$30-60/month

## Nervous System & Breathing

**Evidence: Strong for cortisol-T mechanism, Strong for cyclic sighing**

Cortisol and testosterone are produced from the same precursor (pregnenolone). Chronic stress = elevated cortisol = suppressed T.

**Cyclic Sighing** (double-inhale nose, long slow exhale mouth):
- 2023 Stanford RCT: beat box breathing, cyclic hyperventilation, AND mindfulness meditation
- 5 minutes daily is sufficient

**Box Breathing** (4-4-4-4): also effective. Use before training or in high-stress moments.

**Wim Hof:** activates sympathetic nervous system (cortisol spike). Good for cold tolerance and energy. Do NOT use before bed.

**Workout timing:** Finish training 2-3 hours before bed minimum. 75% of GH released in first 1-2 hours of sleep.

## Red Light Therapy

**Evidence: Moderate for recovery, Weak for testosterone**

Red and near-infrared light boosts mitochondrial ATP production.

- Recovery: measurable reductions in DOMS and inflammation markers. Strongest pre-workout.
- Testosterone: not replicated. Don't buy it for T.
- Skin quality: strong evidence for collagen production.

**Devices:** PlatinumLED BioMax ($300-1,200), Mito Red Light ($200-800, best value), Joovv ($400-1,500+).

**Protocol:** 10-20 min, 6-12 inches from panel, 3-5x/week.

## Competition & The Winner Effect

**Evidence: Strong**

Winners of competitions experience testosterone increases; losers show decreases. Replicated in chess, judo, tennis, video games, and election outcomes.

- Strongest in men with high baseline T and low basal cortisol
- Decisive wins produce the strongest T response
- Symbolic wins count
- Sustained competitive engagement maintains higher baseline T over time

## Fatherhood & Testosterone

**Evidence: Strong for the decline**

- Partnered fathers: 26% decline in morning T, 34% decline in evening T (PNAS 2011, n=624)
- Fathers doing 3+ hours daily childcare had lowest T
- Biological adaptation, not malfunction — promotes bonding, reduces aggression
- Partially reversible after intensive caregiving period
- Exercise, sleep, and dietary strategies partially buffer the decline

## Environmental Toxins

### Highest Priority Actions

1. **Switch cookware** to cast iron or stainless steel (PFAS in non-stick coatings)
2. **Glass food storage** — never microwave in plastic (BPA/phthalates)
3. **Fragrance-free products** — "fragrance" on labels = hidden phthalates
4. **RO water filter** under the sink ($200-400 one-time) — removes 95-99% of pharmaceutical estrogens, PFAS, heavy metals
5. **Organic Dirty Dozen** — strawberries, spinach, peppers, apples most contaminated

## Exercise Snacks

**Evidence: Moderate-Strong for cardiometabolic, Moderate for hormonal**

Brief (5-10 min) vigorous activity bouts distributed through the day.

- Set a timer every 60-90 min: 2 min of stairs, pushups, squats, brisk walk
- Brief resistance bouts cause acute T spikes — multiple daily bouts extend time at elevated T vs. one gym session
- 2025 systematic review: improves glucose control, blood pressure, strength, cognitive function
